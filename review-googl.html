Q4 2025 was Alphabet's cleanest beat in six quarters. Street Q&A — Key Transcript Highlights

Selected analyst questions from the Q4 2025 earnings call (Jan 28, 2026, 4:30 PM ET). Paraphrased for clarity.

Brian Nowak, Morgan Stanley
Q: Can you give us any quantification of how Gemini integration is affecting Search query growth and monetization per query?

A (CEO): Sundar Pichai noted that AI Overviews are now reaching over 1 billion users monthly and that click-through rates on AI-enhanced results are "equivalent to or better than" traditional blue-link results. He declined to quantify specific CPC or CPM impacts but said the team was "very encouraged" by early monetization signals and that the Q4 Search beat was partially attributable to AI-enhanced ad formats.

Mark Mahaney, Evercore ISI
Q: Cloud operating margin hit 17.5% in Q4 — is this a structural level or could there be step-downs as you invest in AI infrastructure?

A (CFO): Anat Ashkenazi said the Q4 Cloud margin of 17.5% was "an important proof point" and that scale leverage is "structural and durable." She guided for continued margin progression in 2026 but cautioned that the pace of improvement may vary quarter to quarter based on infra timing. The implication: Cloud margins are unlikely to decline below Q4 levels barring a significant step-up in infrastructure costs.

Doug Anmuth, J.P. Morgan
Q: On the $70B buyback — how should we think about pacing given you also have a $54B capex commitment in 2026?

A (CFO): Ashkenazi reiterated that with ~$90B of cash on hand and trailing twelve-month FCF of ~$85B, there is ample room to fund both. She said the buyback will be executed "opportunistically" and that the board authorized it specifically because "we believe the stock significantly undervalues the long-term opportunity."
